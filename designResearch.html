A Code of Conduct for Content Aggregators | NYTimes

  • The Golden Rule of Aggregation: "Aggregate others as you would wish to be aggregated yourself."

  • Introduces "The Curator's Code," a site that proposes a new method of attributing sources on the web.


Attribution 101| The Curator's Code

  • Mission: to make the business of attribution more standardized and routine.

  • They have proposed two unicode characters for attributing.

  • A sideways S ("via") denotes a direct repost/link, or a piece of content that came directly from another source.

  • A looped arrow ("hat tip") signals a source that inspired a further thought.

Spotlight on Content Curation | Moz

  • Don't hoard - focus on the information that matters

  • Less is more - trim unnecessary fat, serve only the good stuff

  • Be innovative - dont just copy and paste; be innovative and organize your content in a way that'll attract new readers
